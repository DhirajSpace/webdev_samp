  1. Shop from trusted websites
    Use official stores or well-known e-commerce platforms (Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra).

üïµÔ∏è 2Ô∏è‚É£ Recognizing Fake E-commerce Sites

Fake e-commerce websites are designed to steal money or sensitive information.

üîπ Warning Signs of Fake Sites:
  • URLs that mimic real sites: amaz0n.com, flipkart-shop.net
  • Unrealistically low prices or huge discounts
  • Missing HTTPS or lock icon üîí
  • Poor website layout, grammar, and spelling errors
  • Urgent messages like "Buy now or miss out!"
üîπ How to Protect Yourself:
  • Type the website URL directly instead of clicking on links from emails or ads
  • Search online for reviews or complaints about the site
  • Use credit cards or digital wallets offering buyer protection
  • Enable two-factor authentication on your accounts
üîπ Real-Life Example:

A user finds a laptop at half the price on an unknown website. After purchasing, the laptop never arrives, and the site disappears. This is a classic e-commerce scam.

üîí 3Ô∏è‚É£ Secure Payment Gateways

Payment gateways handle the secure transfer of money online between the buyer, seller, and bank.

üîπ Trusted Gateways:
  • UPI Apps: Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm
  • Digital Wallets: PayPal, Amazon Pay
  • Bank portals: ICICI, SBI, HDFC online banking
üîπ Best Practices:
  1. Look for HTTPS and the lock icon before entering payment information
  2. Avoid using public Wi-Fi for transactions
  3. Enable 2FA / OTP verification whenever possible
  4. Check transaction limits in your banking or UPI app
üîπ Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • Saving card details on multiple apps unnecessarily
  • Clicking unknown payment links from email or chat
  • Using unsecured or outdated apps

üè¶ 4Ô∏è‚É£ Protecting Bank and Card Information

Protecting your bank account and card details is critical to avoid fraud.

üîπ Practical Tips:
  • Never share PIN, CVV, or OTP with anyone, including self-proclaimed bank officials
  • Enable transaction alerts via SMS or email
  • Regularly check statements for unauthorized transactions
  • Avoid storing card information on multiple apps or websites
  • Use unique and strong passwords for online banking and wallet apps
üîπ Warning Signs:
  • Unsolicited calls or messages asking for OTP
  • Pop-ups asking for card details on suspicious websites
  • Requests to transfer money for "verification" or "refund"

üö® 5Ô∏è‚É£ Avoiding UPI & OTP Scams

UPI and OTP scams are among the fastest-growing online frauds in India.

üîπ Common Scams:
  1. Bank Call/OTP Scam: Fraudsters pose as bank officials and ask for OTPs or UPI PINs
  2. Refund Scams: "Click here and enter OTP to receive your refund"
  3. Social Media Scams: Fake giveaways asking for advance payment
  4. Phishing Links: Fake UPI or banking apps that steal credentials
üîπ Safety Practices:
  • Never share OTP, UPI PIN, or card info with anyone
  • Always log in directly through official apps, not through links
  • Verify refund messages directly via official channels
  • Set daily or transaction limits on UPI for extra protection
  • Report fraudulent messages or accounts immediately to your bank or cybercrime portal
üîπ Real-Life Example:

A fraudster messages, "You won ‚Çπ10,000! Enter OTP to claim." The user enters OTP, and the fraudster immediately transfers money from their account.
‚úÖ Correct action: Ignore, block, and report the scam.
