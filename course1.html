Online threats come in many forms. Knowing them helps you avoid falling into traps.

‚û°Ô∏è Example: A fake bank email asking you to "verify your account."

üîπ 2. Malware & Viruses

Software designed to damage your device or steal your data.

‚û°Ô∏è Example: Downloading infected files or apps from untrusted sources.

üîπ 3. Identity Theft

üîπ 4. Cyberbullying

üîπ 5. Online Scams & Frauds

üîπ 6. Fake News & Misinformation

ü§ù 4. Cyber Ethics and Responsible Online Behavior

Cyber Ethics means using the internet in a respectful, honest, and lawful way. It is about being a responsible digital citizen who respects others' privacy and rights.

üß≠ Golden Rules of Cyber Ethics
  1. Respect Privacy: Never share someone's personal info or photos without permission
  2. Be Kind: Avoid rude comments, trolling, or hate speech
  3. Don't Plagiarize: Give credit to original creators ‚Äî don't copy or steal online content
  4. Stay Legal: Avoid downloading pirated movies, games, or software
  5. Think Before Posting: Once online, always online ‚Äî even deleted posts can be recovered
  6. Report Misuse: If you see cyberbullying or fake news, report it instead of ignoring it

üåü Being ethical online builds a safer and more respectful digital world.

üë£ 5. Digital Footprint: What It Is and How It Affects You

Your Digital Footprint is the trail of data you leave every time you go online ‚Äî from your Google searches and social media likes to your emails and photos.

üß© Types of Digital Footprints
  • Active Footprint: Information you intentionally share ‚Äî posts, comments, profiles
  • Passive Footprint: Data collected automatically ‚Äî browsing history, cookies, location
üö¶ Why It Matters
  • Employers, colleges, and others can see your online activity
  • Oversharing can lead to identity theft or misuse of information
  • Once shared online, information can spread and stay forever
‚úÖ Tips to Manage Your Digital Footprint
  • Review and tighten privacy settings on social media
  • Avoid sharing sensitive information publicly
  • Delete unused accounts or old posts
  • Use strong passwords and log out from public devices
  • Regularly Google your name to see what appears about you online

üîç Remember: "The Internet never forgets." Post only what you're proud to keep forever.
